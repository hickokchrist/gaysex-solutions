<p>
<a href="https://freeformportland.org" target="_blank">Freeform Portland</a> called for some art submissions for their summer fundraising campaign.
</p>
<p>
Be sure to donate to support community radio! Freeform Portland is a non-commercial entity run entirely by volunteers, so, I volunteered to try to make some art!
</p>
<p>
It was a lot of fun, there were some principles set forth by the design committee, but I mostly felt like I was free to take the reigns with whatever vision my brain cooked up.
</p>
<p>
This was also a great excuse to learn how to use Blender a little more. One exciting accident happened while configuring the fanned laser array. I stumbled on a setting which turned the lasers into a diffuse flat sheet, looking very similar to real lasers I've seen against fog! That was an immediate "yes, and"...
</p>
<p>
You can see this discovery occur in the gif animation further down the page!
</p>

<p>
Not having any background in design or digital media, I reached out to a rhythm gamer collective for some advice. The issue I was running into was that the lighting that made the background look good also made the text look awful. And vice-versa.
</p>
<p>
I was given the brilliantly-simple (meaning, it never would have come to mind naturally) advice to just render the background separately from the text and put it together in post-production. D'uh!
</p>

<p>
Too married to the idea of a shiny metallic brand logo against a loud laser-filled background, I finally settled on something which doesn't seem like it looks too bad.
</p>
<p>
After all those renders, what ended up working was a simple, semi-transparent blue logo against the final scene in various aspect ratios.
</p>
<p>
Oh, also the fact that there's a singularity next door, it felt appropriate to give the logo some fun warp so as to imply some kind of dynamic action.
</p>

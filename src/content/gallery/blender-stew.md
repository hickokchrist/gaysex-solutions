<p>
What good is a radio show without marketing?
</p>
<p>
A vision came to me, of my stage name in inflated mylar balloon letters. Finally! A vision for Blender.
</p>
<p>
Iteration after iteration, painstaking lighting tweaks, cloth simulations, inflation simulations... It was a neat process. My poor li'l Microsoft Surface Pro 11 would actually bluescreen/restart during the Blender render unless I aimed a box fan at it. Yes it's the ARM installation lol.
</p>
<p>
The idea was to have the balloon letters over some kind of a gaping hole. Given the brand "Sonic Stew", a cauldron/stew pot seemed perfect! I considered what to make of the scene, and toyed with some backgrounds. To make things more fun, I learned how to project some of the <a href="/photospheres/pinemountain2018" target="_blank">photospheres</a> I've taken onto a big (well, variable-sized, really) sphere in the background of the scene. That gave me a lot of leeway with perspectives. 
</p>
<p>
Making the cauldron was cool, I simply drew a curve and rotated that around the z-axis. Thank you, Math 252, for teaching me the concept of creating solids by rotating curves. I created the most cursed rim possible on the cauldron, just because I can. Unfortunately, smoothing out the geometry from merging the handles onto the frame also smoothed out that awful bowl lip.
</p>
<p>
I had to figure out how to blur the background. Volumetric fog was unironically an option, but I chose to simulate a cloth curtain instead, blow some wind against it, then freeze that solid and make it my translucent backdrop. That was so much fun to think about and approach!
</p>
<p>
Because the process of sculpting/inflating the balloon letters was so time intensive (mostly the rendering), and combined with the fact that I want my stage name to contrast with every other text in frame, I had to figure out what to do with the rest of the text styling. One friend (are coworkers actually friends?) One future enemy sent a pic of the og power rangers logo, emphasizing the 3d rendered text curved around the center lightning bolt, and I was sold. There's no bolt in my adaptation, I certainly didn't need that to strenghten my iconography.
</p>
<p>
After much deliberation and struggle, I decided on a natural background I captured at Pine Mountain, Oregon in 2018. Then, after a bunch of renders, I realized I needed to add marketing to my logo and make it a whole poster!
</p>

<p>
My social media algorithm has convinced me to seek out freshly-milled flour for its nutritional content lol. On further checking, it seems like there might be a lot I can get out of real, fresh flour. Let's give it a shot!
</p>
<p>
One of the weekends I went out to the waterfront to do some blogging and work on my radio show, I checked out the farmer's market to see if any of the bakeries use fresh flour. I got in touch with Freehand Bakery and learned they do pre-orders for freshly-milled flour! I chose a hard red wheat flour, because my goal is to make a crusty loaf of hearth bread.
</p>
<p>
Most of my new flour is packed away in containers in the freezer for storage, but I had a little bit I wanted to use right away. I almost made cookies (but I've been overdosing on chocolate chip cookies for the last couple weeks) and decided to knock out a loaf today (Sunday). While the dough was proofing during its several resting phases, I had enough flour in the bottom of the bag to make some biscuits. I grated up some cheese and mixed up a batch of those right quick.
</p>
<p>
Okay, so, as cool as all that sounds, I have a habit of not touching my phone when I'm cooking (because phones are filthy) so I don't have any action shots... just the <a href="https://www.tiktok.com/@bulbouscorm/video/7628072987069123854" target="_blank">results</a>. 
</p>

April 6 was a momentous day! I had my debut radio program, <a href="/sonic-stew" target="_blank">Sonic Stew</a>, and it went without catastrophe while being a lot of fun! 

It's very touching to hear my friends and family say they liked this-or-that song, or make inside jokes referencing stuff I said over the air. Thank you so much, dear reader, for being part of that process as well!

The debut show felt stressful yet natural, my primary concern was making sure my voice was at a high enough level! After some blog updates and website maintenance, I'll be working on my next show, airing April 20!

</div>

The rest of the week gave us incredible sunshine. I suppose it's been almost a month of bright weather at this point.

I challenged myself to ride my bicycle whenever possible. Run an errand down the road? Ride my bike. Craving a cheeseburger? Ride my bike. One day I rode from Hazel Dell down to the waterfront and back home, it was about 15 miles in total. There are some nasty hills in Hazel Dell too, which have been a barrier for me. It felt great to finally get over that.

In one of my previous blog posts I raved about biking down Columbia street, well I obviously did that again! A few more times, in fact. During a journey along the waterfront, I think  I found the most popular running spot in all of Vancouver, possibly at the most popular running time. I passed hundreds of joggers along the waterfront trail betewen the I-5 bridge east past McMenamins. On my way back, I detoured around the fort and library, then trekked back up town home. I biked nearly 40 miles this week.

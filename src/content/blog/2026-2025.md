**Early 2025 - The Meh-st of Times**

l\[0pt\]0.3 ![image](start.jpg){width="30%"}

2024 ended with an unfortunate beard trimmer accident which forced the removal of my load-bearing mustache. With the jumbo XL runway situated between my nostrils and upper lip exposed bare, I was forced to reckon with...all that, and the fact that I was in critical need of a haircut. This also played into deleting my social media. I felt like kind of a joke between my appearance and my immature behavior online.

In the midst of feeling all that, my beloved friends and I were going to dinner at Amaro's Table or Raven's Manor or Indian Curry pizza, scheduling movie nights to watch that *awful* Section 31 movie, or going to men's gatherings. At this time, I kind of got into the newest Forza video game because I was able to drive a sport coupe version of my car, that was awesome! Then elon musk did two nazi salutes at the presidential inauguration, remember that? Remember his little grunts? He threw those arms with all his heart, indeed.

One cool project I completed was due to the looming tiktok ban. I spent an evening following some instructions and I downloaded just about every video I've ever liked or favorited, if it still exists. I'm not going to get *Vined* again. It sounds stupid, and it is, because there are so many stupid memes and subcultures I've discovered through tiktok. I wanted to hold on to a piece of those memories in anticipation of the fabled ban. Then the ban lasted about one night's sleep. Anyway, hit me up for the google drive link for many gigabytes of funny tiktoks. And, like, follow me?!

Baby, that's just January. Buckle up!\

The plan is the same when musk's time comes: to go to my car, open the doors and max volume blast the playist I made while I spray Martinelli's cider in the air and whoop. March started strong, I took a monumental trip down to Junction City to see my dear friends Kacey and Rachael. They're the best, we got a karaoke room and had a grand time with our group of seven. That trip was significant for our friend group, it set in motion a lot of the dynamics that made us stronger friends today.

I appear to be a little obsessed with music, because guess what? Christopher's next highlight is having seen Rebecca Black on March 5 at the Hawthorne Theatre. It was one of the **sickest** live performances I've ever seen, her energy is amazing! She's genuinely talented, please check out her album Salvation.

Due to the fact that I had caught COVID at the Moda Center in October 2024 while seeing Charli XCX and Troye Sivan, I'm a masked concert hoe now. Sometimes I forget, though something is better than nothing.

Winter subsided with more visits to friends in and around town. We met for dinner, to catch up, just hang out. Given that I am solar-powered, it felt good to be on the other side of the dark part of the year, so I started getting out of the house more.

My buddy Marc (Mark with a "C?" Cark?) celebrated a birthday with a big group at an Indian restaurant in Vancouver days after the Rebecca Black concert (listen to her album yet?) I got to see several folks for the first time since hibernating for winter, and meet some lovely new people who have since become friends! Hi Tim!

Another close friend Clark celebrated his birthday in March, we went out to Mexican food in Woodland to celebrate, it was a blast!

His friend hosts a regular time slot and was suddenly unavailable that night. DJ "Bulbous" Corm made his debut on the air that night. April arrives! The OBT sends me a killer coupon, so I buy a ticket to a ballet about Marilyn Monroe for 40% off, lmao. That was fun! The barbiturate overdose scene was a little too abstract for me.

My lovely pal Billy reaches out to arrange a hike. He took me out the gorge, I had never been to Multnomah Falls. The Earth is so horny out there, it's just wet and squirting everywhere.

We gathered as a family for Easter, I got to see basically my whole family in Lebanon (Oregon...) for the first time since Christmas. I am blessed to live close to my family to see my parents, four siblings, and basically their entire families at least a few times per year. Just solo me, lmao!

Ziwe toured and came to Portland to perform at the Aladdin Theater at the end of April. I had a great time, laughed so much. Check her out on youtube!

The first weekend in May was another trip to Junction City to see Kacey and Rachael. I did my first karaoke performance of a Bad Bunny song, and that was another weekend some stuff went down that brought us closer as friends.

May continues, and I decided to try out Amtrak! Within three weeks of each other, I'd planned a trip to Seattle as well as Junction City again. I needed new brakes on my car, so I was driving light until that got done in June. The first journey was a testbed for the second train trip, choo choo!

Alright *henny*, we've made it to the end of May! My big train plan was to take my bicycle down to Eugene to ride around the river or some of my favorite spots around town for the first time since 2021. I had booked a ticket for myself and my +1 (did you know you had to buy a ticket for your bike) and I was incredibly nervous about the actual mechanics of taking your bike on the train. So, I left my apartment with a backpack and my bicycle, rode the bus to Portland, rode the train to Eugene, and my buddy Kacey picked me up to stay the weekend in Junction City. There was a random car show in Junction City that weekend too, so we walked by on our way to get coffee that Saturday morning.

The weather was beautiful, we got to bike along my favorite trail along the pussy lips of the Willamette, straddling the city of Eugene. We went to Saturday Market after that, and I was minding my damn bike in the huge crowd the whole time lmao. The rest of the weekend was awesome as ever with my friends the Bells (Kacey and Rachael and their kids). When I took the train back to Portland to go home that Sunday, I got to find out that commuting to Vancouver on public transit is much smoother during the weekdays! I sure wish there were light rail connecting Portland to Vancouver.

The very next weekend we traded places, and Kacey and Rachael came up to Vancouver to visit me! The plan was to see a concert in Ridgefield just a couple miles up the freeway from me. We were able to check out the Salmon Creek bike path near my place in our leisure time, and enjoyed the hell out of the Sessanta show! Puscifer, Primus, and A Perfect Circle played a legendary show, my high-school self was pissing inside, since I never thought I'd see some of those songs performed live! What a blast. The day I bid farewell to Kacey and Rachael to set off back home, my buddies Patrick and Shane and I went to Rooster Rock to soak up the sun on the river.

Speaking of rivers, once May hit and the sun was with the PNW, I got annual passes for the river beaches by me. After work when the mood struck, I would drive out to Sauvie Island or east to Rooster Rock to get some vitamin D and read. I finished an obituary on Henry Kissinger, what a ghoul. Turns out he's had a direct hand in just about any state-level mass killing you can name in the last 70 years. Fuck that guy.

Second weekend in June? What would I do but DRIVE DOWN TO EUGENE again. Actually, it was Cottage Grove. Fun fact, I recently realized that I've never driven further south than Cottage Grove, having only been licensed to drive since 2021. My amazing friend Matthew celebrated their birthday with a big party, which I always adore! This year was a 90's themed bash, we had tons of fun.

The following weekend, my friends Polly Glot, Josh, and I went to Fathom PDX. It was an interactive art exhibit, not unlike MeowWolf's endeavors, but smaller in scale. It looks like the artist collective who ran it, Roboto Octopodo, is in its early stages still. Exciting!

That was my "rest" weekend, because the final weekend in June had me hosting my head bee Autumn from Seattle. She's my bestie from uni, shoot, almost ten years ago she first approached me in the library and asked if I wanted to work on homework with her group. We had a lot of fun in Vancouver, the weather was excellent so I got to take her to the river. We ran into my friend Tim at the beach, hi Tim!

Wow, that was a whole page, just for June! Look, I don't have a medium to share all this information like a normal person. Just kidding, nothing is normal about social media. I digress.


JULY! My amazing friend Jessica planned a camping trip for her birthday out near Oakridge. It was going to be Jessica, her husband Tim and daughter, plus our friends Bec and Alex. Imagine getting food poisoning and missing your own birthday trip, yikes :( The five of us had the best time we could and got to bond in a new way. I definitely want to start going camping more. On the way home from Oakridge, I managed to attend my nephew's eighth birthday party.

<strong><h3 id="early">Early 2025 - The Meh-st of Times</h3></strong>
<div class="float-wrap">
 <div class="img-item float-left" style="max-width: 400px;">

2024 ended with an unfortunate beard trimmer accident which forced the removal of my load-bearing mustache. With the jumbo XL runway situated between my nostrils and upper lip exposed bare, I was forced to reckon with...all that, and the fact that I was in critical need of a haircut. This also played into deleting my social media. I felt like kind of a joke between my appearance and my immature behavior online.

In the midst of feeling all that, my beloved friends and I were going to dinner at Amaro's Table or Raven's Manor or Indian Curry pizza, scheduling movie nights to watch that *awful* Section 31 movie, or going to men's gatherings. At this time, I kind of got into the newest Forza video game because I was able to drive a sport coupe version of my car, that was awesome! Then elon musk did two nazi salutes at the presidential inauguration, remember that? Remember his little grunts? He threw those arms with all his heart, indeed.

One cool project I completed was due to the looming tiktok ban. I spent an evening following some instructions and I downloaded just about every video I've ever liked or favorited, if it still exists. I'm not going to get *Vined* again. It sounds stupid, and it is, because there are so many stupid memes and subcultures I've discovered through tiktok. I wanted to hold on to a piece of those memories in anticipation of the fabled ban. Then the ban lasted about one night's sleep. Anyway, hit me up for the google drive link for many gigabytes of funny tiktoks. And, like, follow me?!

Baby, that's just January. Buckle up!

The plan is the same when musk's time comes: to go to my car, open the doors and max volume blast the playist I made while I spray Martinelli's cider in the air and whoop. Might spawn a party, might get shot, I live in kind of a hick town. Did I text you and ask for song suggestions for the dead trump playlist? Well, send 'em! Martinelli's on standby o7

March started strong, I took a monumental trip down to Junction City to see my dear friends Kacey and Rachael. They're the best, we got a karaoke room and had a grand time with our group of seven. That trip was significant for our friend group, it set in motion a lot of the dynamics that made us stronger friends today.

I appear to be a little obsessed with music, because guess what? Christopher's next highlight is having seen Rebecca Black on March 5 at the Hawthorne Theatre. It was one of the **sickest** live performances I've ever seen, her energy is amazing! She's genuinely talented, please check out her album Salvation.

Due to the fact that I had caught COVID at the Moda Center in October 2024 while seeing Charli XCX and Troye Sivan, I'm a masked concert hoe now. Sometimes I forget, though something is better than nothing.

</div>

---

Winter subsided with more visits to friends in and around town. We met for dinner, to catch up, just hang out. Given that I am solar-powered, it felt good to be on the other side of the dark part of the year, so I started getting out of the house more.

My buddy Marc (Mark with a "C?" Cark?) celebrated a birthday with a big group at an Indian restaurant a few days after the Rebecca Black concert (listen to her album yet?) I got to see several folks for the first time since hibernating for winter, and meet some lovely new people who have since become friends! Hi Tim!

Another close friend Clark celebrated his birthday in March, we went out to Mexican food in Woodland to celebrate, it was a blast!

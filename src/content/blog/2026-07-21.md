<h3>FRIDAY</h3>
<p>
Preparing for that weekend, I was hosting two friends from Seattle visiting for pride festivities. Work was a slog, but after my shift was over my dear friends Marc and Jon had invited me for dinner. Jon roasted a pork shoulder in the oven all day and it was delicious! A couple bites into the salad I had intrusive thoughts about cyclospora and couldn't finish the greens. Marc and Jon were hosting several of our friends for a movie night, but I could not stay since I was meeting my guests and we were all going out to dance.
</p>
<div class="img-item">

![](/images/roast.webp)
*Pork Shoulder*

</div>
<p>
We went to Realm for Wreckno’s Big Gay Soirée. For the first time since I've been there, they had the upstairs open. It was really fun to wander between stages to vibe on the different dance floors. At one point, my group ran into one of the alumni from the popular drag queen TV show, and there's a pic somewhere with me present hehe.
</p>
<p>
At the Big Gay Soirée, many of our friends were in town and dancing with us. We dance all night, the party is insanely energetic, one of the best shows I've ever attended. At the end of the night, we actually head over to Bearracuda. I've only been to one, in 2023, and it was just a big dancefloor on the ground level. This time I went upstairs and the space is really cool. The dance floor was sparse as the night was ending, but we ran into some friendly faces of the group, and suddenly this hot daddy is taking an interest in me. Brian is his name, very handsome man, I'm flattered and speechless and feeling overwhelmed (in a good way). Hot Brian from Bearracuda gestured to his partner who happens to be deaf and I gush about how useful ASL is in the club.
</p>
<p>
Still at Bearracuda, I see one of my dear friend's handsome visage peeking out the railing upstairs. He lives in Seattle, so literally any chance I see him I have to go give him a hug. I sneak in real quick, smack his jockstrap-bound ass, kiss him, say hi, then say "ok I'm gonna stop kiki-ing in the darkroom, bye!" and zoom off 😂
<p>
I retire alone at home in my quarters with my two guests in the living room, and eventually slept for an hour or two.
</p>

<h3>SATURDAY</h3>
<p>
Some things came to a head and I made a choice to exit a situation I'd entered recently. Not maliciously, with all love, Christopher needs to take care of himself.
The next day, I went out for some breakfast at my traditional bagel spot. This was the first time I'd driven there from my house, it was kind of sad.
</p>

<div class="float-wrap">
<div class="img-item float-right">

![](/images/pride-pool.webp)
*After the party*

<p>
Saturday's plan was a pool party for which many of my Seattle friends came to town to attend. I rallied my mental health and resolved to have the best possible time. The timing couldn't have worked 
out better for me to have exited my situation and resumed life as a free agent. Hot Brian from Bearracuda was at the party, so I got to chat with him more. In fact, I had the chance to 
socialize with a lot of new people to me who had familiar faces due to attending events around town. I got to know a ton of my newer friends in a <s>w</s>hole new context, it was amazing (seven amazings). 
Hole McJail felt like his true self again.
</p>

<p>
The pool party had someone DJing too! I asked about what software and control deck he was using, but honestly I can't remember what he said. The gracious host had arranged for plenty of food and beverages, but your favorite blogger hates alcohol so I was excited to find the jugs of icy water. I ate one of the last cheeseburgers and nibbled on some fruit platters too. 
I was able to make friends in the shade, since the shadowy real estate was a hot (rather cool) commodity. I chatted with some friends I hadn't seen in a while, as well as meet their partners. The pool party came to a close, and I wanted to continue the fun. Most of my friends were going to Gaylabration and I went home to change, rally, buy tickets, and head back out.
</p>

</div>

<p>
It was after the start of the show, by one minute, when I went to buy tickets online and sales had closed. Luckily, I reached out to my raver fam group chat, JC was able to confirm they did door sales for me cuz he's an angel. Gaylabration (Radiance) was held at Crystal Ballroom. In the crowd, I saw my favorite meteorologist and his equally famous partner. I don't know them so I didn't approach them or anything but it was cool. This was another night of multiple dance floors with multiple DJs playing at once. I'm new to all this, so I don't really know that's like, a normal thing. It was really cool to wander to the different floor, and guess who but DJ Sappho was spinning. At one point I was about to leave and popped in to them spinning a Bad Bunny song, and I just about died. That made me stay a little while longer for sure!
We danced all night once again. A couple PDX pals were around too, but oddly I have more pride family in Seattle than Portland? Oh, d'uh, the raver ones. I don't have rave fam in Portland, really. Yet. Oh, and of course, my favorite gogo dancer was dancing once again! We love Jojo!
</p>
<p>
One of the coolest parts of attending Gaylabration was the fact that I met so many new friends at the pool party that day, to run into once again on the dance floor. It's really neat when you make fast friends at parties and then see them again at another event! Friends also made a point to pull me aside and compliment me on my radio show, they really listened! I watched my buddy brag to his DJ pup about how I have good taste in music, sorry I'm just over here trying to not let my ego get too big haha. And while I was on the floor? I received a notification that I had a new follower on my Mixcloud account where my radio episodes are posted.
</p>
<p>
After everything, it also felt really good when people would recognize me and come say hi on the dance floor. I adore all my friends, and thank you for making me feel like I belong.
</div>

<h3>Sunday</h3>
<p>
At 10am I had planned a brunch date with one of my friends Holly I've known from high school. We caught up, I had TONS of stories to tell her. Too many, in fact, for one meal! We discussed how she's keeping her family busy during summer, as well as some TV show recommendations! Dear reader, please forgive me for not knowing about the show Hacks before now! I love Meg Stalter!
</p>
<p>
That morning, it was time to bid farewell to my houseguests. Those poor souls, I was so full of executive dysfunction that I barely had a functional toilet for them to use. (I'm exaggerating!) They were lovely for the two nights I hosted them, and I appreciate their company when I needed to talk to someone.
</p>
<p>
The rest of the day was spent preparing for the final two dance parties of the weekend. The first was called LA VISTA, at a venue I'd never been to before (so, most venues) called Wonderlove. It was billed as a rooftop Reggaeton party, and, no need to say any more cuz I'm there.
Closing out the night would be Yes, Sir which was hosted at Realm. If I told you there were gogo dancers performing, would you guess which one I went to see? I actually did spend a few hours making some more <a href="/gallery/polymer-flops" target="_blank">Floptropican kandi</a> to commemorate the occasions.

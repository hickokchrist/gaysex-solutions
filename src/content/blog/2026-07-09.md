<h2>Life after Beyond</h2>
<p>
After the music festival, my neurotransmitters were shot. I *ugly-cried* on the car ride home, mostly because it was like eight hours long 😜
</p>
<p>
Ever since launching this website, my life has felt like it's got an endless stream of projects on my plate. This is very intentional, and working as expected, in the sense that, omg my mental health loves a task. The radio station for which I volunteer is starting a fundraising campaign next week, so I've been working on some <a href="/gallery/ffp-fundraiser" target="_blank">digital designs</a> for marketing media. That process was a lot of fun, I felt like I learned a great deal in a short amount of time. Given that <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endometriosis" target="_blank">I can never bear children</a>, it is so satisfying to be able to see an idea of mine turn into reality. The radio station also called for all DJs to submit a prerecorded promotional message about the fundraiser. One of the ones I turned in sounded pretty boss! Donate now at <a href="https://freeformportland.org" target="_blank">FreeformPortland.org</a>.
</p>
<h2>Enough with all that creativity</h2>
<p>
Aside from all that digital creation,  I had to dance. I went to my first Tabor Dance in Portland just after returning from Beyond Wonderland. That was fun! But it was very packed in the indoor venue. Fortunately for me, there was a Reggaeton Rave scheduled for a Friday I was free. Unfortunately, nobody was in town to go with me, but you know I got there early and closed the night out. When I got there, only three-or-so couples were dancing on the floor, while about 30 people were upstairs by the bathroom or outside smoking. Not to toot my own horn, but once I stood in the middle and started shaking my booty, people started packing in. While I was standing in the middle, I thought it was kind of weird that people (girls) were coming and standing close to me. I saw one muscle cutie looking uncomfortable but alone, and he is indeed straight. At the end of the night, there was another cute guy who was living his life on the floor. He is also straight. But that's ok, most guys are! 
</p>
<p>
The Reggaeton Rave was so much fun, I danced for hours and hours, they played <a href="https://www.tiktok.com/@bulbouscorm/video/7658568370769415438" target="_blank">Bad Bunny and Young Miko</a> and also a lot of more classic rave music (Woops, Diet Coke).
</p>

<h2>Oh and then it was July 4 or whatever</h2>
<p>
I went to a pool party, as I am wont to do, and had a really good time! I met a lot of cool new people, and saw many familiar faces. That's pretty much all I did that day, to be honest my knees were still hurting from the music festival and the Reggaeton Rave.
</p>
<p>
Ever since then, I've pretty much been finishing the audiovisual media for the radio station, oh and updating this website! I added some new project pages, new photospheres, and of course these blog posts. While working on this, I decided to add a feature allowing the user to click on any non-thumbnail photo to view the full size photo. Full size is a strange term given that I intentionally downsample my photos for hosting on the web, BUT, let's just say all ya'll viewing this site on mobile are getting a diminished experience. Well, slightly less diminished now!
</p>

What a beautiful bounty of sunshine the last week has given us. While I'm really dreading the upcoming fire season, one can't help but soak up the rays.

Catching up on the week, Monday was spent visiting some friends to drop off cookies I baked :)

Chewsday (innit?) my pal hit me up to walk downtown to get some pizza cuz his spouse was out of town for work. We had such a good time! Getting out in the sunshine probably inspired me to keep that pushing throughout the week.

Wednesday, I recruited a couple friends to attend the Freeform Portland tenth anniversary party. It's such a lovely community of people passionate about music, I'm very excited for my debut on Monday. The Freeform party was so fun, I got to catch up with some awesome folks I met during training - both trainees and seasoned DJ's. 

Thursday I met up with a new friend I'd met at a few events, who recognized me! This might be one of the first successful uses of my business card lmfaoooooo 

Friday was my 'rest evening', I gave myself permission to lock in on my database project and create my mix for the radio show I'm hosting on Monday. What I did end up doing was doomscrolling in bed after riding my bike, like, eight miles down the road and back, which included some intense hill climbing. 

Saturday, my quest was to go back to the Vancouver farmer's market to get some delicious bread. This time, I decided to take my bicycle. After a certain point, I did get to coast downhill for pretty much the whole second half. I gotta say, riding my bicycle south down Columbia street in Vancouer struck me with beauty and serenity of the whole scene. The coolest part was seeing the (hideous) I-5 bridge cresting over the roadway in front of me. If you like to bicycle, ride south on Columbia in Vancouver from like, 45th ave all the way to the waterfront. It's incredibly beautiful - with the right weather - and basically all on a gentle downslope.

April has been a lot of fun! Lots going on. On April 18, Freeform Portland hosted an open house event for the radio station. I showed up with a cube of drinks to share and asked where I could help out. Being beautifully sunny, I was able to get my straw hat and sit outside welcoming people to the event. I got to meet so many folks I'd only seen online, it was so much fun! 

April 20 was the second episode of my show, <a href="/sonic-stew" target="_blank">Sonic Stew</a>, but there was an event I wanted to attend! Luckily, I had the option to do both. I learned how to properly use Audacity to cut together the song mix and my voiceovers for the two-hour block, and uploaded that to the automated system for my timeslot. The event was an airing of one of my <a href="https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/smiley_face" target="_blank">favorite movies</a> at the Hollywood Theatre in Portland. My buddies and I had a great time at the movie!

The next day ended up being the most perfect Saturday I could have engineered outside of some kind of vacation or trip. It started out with breakfast cooked by my buddy Jon. I decided to ride my bike over, so that I could keep riding around town after our meal. 

I ended up on a 22-mile journey along some accidentally dangerous routes that powered my legs by terror uphill on Evergreen Blvd to Andresen (locals are gonna scream). I wanted to explore the waterfront and see how far East I could go. It was beautiful but made me consider what kind of local government people I need to lobby to build a proper bike path eastward to the Glenn Jackson Bridge. I hooked around up north to the Burnt Bridge Creek trail, which I had explored a lot when I first moved to town. There's nothing quite like building continuity for places by getting their using your own two legs.  

</div>
On my way home, about a mile away from my front door, my legs started cramping. That's honestly something new to me, and it was kind of terrifying realizing that I couldn't stand up to pedal. I was frantically considering if I'd have to crawl up the stairs and ask a neighbor for help carrying my bike, but everything ended up fine lmao.

I was so pooped, I decided to go to a men's club with a hot tub and saunas where my friends from breakfast were also headed. After losing a round of fort night, I headed to the spa where I made a beeline to the hot tub for a good soak. I had a blast or four and headed home. But first! I wanted a plate dinner, as in, Hawaiian teriyaki chicken \& rice \& macaroni salad. I headed to a place called the My-oh-my that featured the food I wanted, and walked into an active Karaoke scene lol. While waiting for food, I hyped myself up to sign up to sing a song and screamed some Bad Bunny at the poor crowd. With that, I handed my card to a cute drunk guy who confirmed he was single, and headed home.

Time to write some resumes, at this moment I'm actually writing this blog post instead of applying for some goldilocks positions I saw around town, haha. Wish me discipline and progress!

<h2>Sausage Party Weekend!</h2>
<h3>Friday: The Day Before</h3>
<p>
Hello again, dear readers! I'm currently sitting passenger in a car on my way home from Seattle. Another trip in the books! This one ended up with some fun surprises along the way.
</p>
<p>
The trip was planned with my friends Marc, Jon, and Bellamy. Our friends in Seattle were hosting a backyard BBQ shindig they host annually. Last year's theme was luau, it was featured in my <a href="/blog/2026-2025" target="_blank">2025 recap newsletter</a>. This year, Marc and Bellamy were able to join us for the trip. We were also celebrating Jon's birthday! Ageless though he is, we love to celebrate an occasion!
</p>
<p>
Friday, the boys picked me up and we jetted off to Seattle by car, vroom vroom! Immediately on opening my door, I find a perler amulet hung on my doorknob with some trinkets, a note, and a kandi bracelet attached! I noticed a car in my apartment complex had some EDM stickers, so I made a kandi bracelet with Floptropican beads and spelled out "ROSE🌸TOY" and gently put it on their side mirror with a note welcoming them to FLoptropica with the bracelet and my name/unit #. That was about a week ago, what a surprise to see that just at that moment! 
</p>
<p>
After picking up a cake at the Red Ribbon bakery at SCM, we checked in to the hotel and walked to Biang Biang in Capitol Hill for dinner. After bringing our leftovers back, I had plans to meet up with a friend who lives in the neighborhood. My traveling companions were pooped and decided to stay back at the hotel while I went out to Diesel. I got there super early, and am not much of a drinker, so I literally just sat and watched people for an hour like a weirdo lol. It was neat, I learned that everyone crowds the bartender closest to the door, so there's less of a wait if you just walk five feet. I got to use that tip the following night to help my friend get served immediately while a big line formed.
</p>
<p>
Anyway, so I was sitting there alone and drinkless like a weirdo, but here's the thing. I kept open body language the whole time, and I stayed off my phone.
</p>
<p>
The night was awesome because I thought I was going to see one friend and do some catching up after <a href="/blog/2026July8" target="_blank">Beyond</a> but he literally showed up with a whole entourage of folks I camped with at the festival. It was amazing, and we literally just stood outside - not even smoking - chatting until like 1am. I had such a blast seeing, like, ten of my Beyond fam. 
</p>
<p>
I have kind of a distinct look, so there was at least one person at Diesel who saw the weirdo sitting by himself switch to a totally different mode, chopping it up with *their* friends.
</p>

<h3>Saturday Night</h3>
<p>
This weekend I avoided making any plans ahead of time because I wanted to see what my party I traveled with would get up to. Friday night they stayed in, and I wanted to suggest some options for Saturday and found out some of my rave fam in Seattle were going to see Marie Vaunt that night. Tickets were $30 so I pretty much instantly bought that and sent the link to Marc, Jon, and Bellamy in case they were interested. That turned an already fantastic weekend into something incredible, honestly.
</p>
<p>
The universe brought us together, truly! It felt like the most perfect coincidence, but all told none of these things were unlikely on their own. It just feels good when things line up and you get to see folks you didn't think you'd see for a while!
</p>

If there's one thing I can do well, it's audit my needs and physiological state. Meeting my 10,000 step goal 90 minutes after midnight is on track to be some kind of record. My knees and hips and back still hurt a little, and boy was I stretching every opportunity I could! It's a bit embarassing, but the stretch that alleviates the most pain is a simple toe-touch. It's not really fun or cool to do that out in public. Especially on the streets of Seattle or Portland! It feels so good standing there with my arms dangling over my feet, but I feel self-conscious and don't want to hold the pose for too long. Not to mention I have to find some kind of wall to face, yikes.
</p>
<p>
Nevertheless, I jumped, jived, and wailed til the lights came on at 2am. My tank was mercilessly soaked through with sweat, I took it off although it made little difference. At one point in the crowd, a group of shirtless muscle twinks appeared next to me. I took my tank back off to close out the night and match vibes, and I think they started to use a couple of my little moves - it was cute!

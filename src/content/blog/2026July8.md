<h2>Damn, you're still alive?</h2>
<p>
Hello, dear reader. It's been a while! I've been up to a lot, A LOT!!! It's to the point that I will have to make a retrospective post covering the months of May and June because they were so dang eventful!
</p>
<p>
But this post is written for a pacific event, that is to say, Beyond Wonderland Pacific Northwest 😜
</p>
<p>
My new rave family invited me to join their camping group for Beyond this year at The Gorge. I did some maffs and crunched some numbers and was able to treat myself to what ended up being the best days of my life.
</p>

<p>
The first couple days were so chill, it was really nice to adjust to camp life. There were heated showers and bathrooms with A/C, so it was pretty bougie if I'm honest.
</p>
<p>
Before the trip, I was able to make kandi bracelets with everyone's name spelled out. They were made with my custom <a href="/gallery/polymer-flops" target="_blank">Floptropican beads</a>, allowing me to induct about 30 new citizens of Floptropica. As well as the random flop phrases I made that weren't personalized. I gave plenty out to PLUR folks, as well as some take-one-leave-one stands. I believe people most appreciated when I would give them my custom Floptropican polymer clay beads, handfuls were given out to many folks.
</p>
<p>
Some of the folks who answered in the "affirmative" to whether they are comfortable with their image being hosted on a website with such a scandalous domain are featured below and throughout the post.

<p>
Once the festival started properly, I was in for such a treat. To be completely honest, I'm brand new to the EDM scene (literally my last blog post was "mY fIrSt RaVe!") so I didn't know ANY of these artists going in. In a way, that's kind of fun in and of itself because I get to form memories and associations with this experience with these artists. And that's exactly what happened.
</p>
<p>
My friends had their artists they wanted to see, and I went with them to see some wub-wubs, but the most fun I had in the musical sense was just wandering off by myself and checking out the different stages on my own. My body literally woke up and started moving - arguably for the first time in my life - when I ran into an artist called 1991 playing some sick UK Drum n' Bass.
</p>

<h2>Why do they all play the same songs?</h2>
<p>
One time, at leadership camp in high school, there were approximately twelve teams of all of us and we had to compete in some kind of skit/sketch performance. Somehow, either by pure psychological emergence or the spreading of words, about seven of the twelve teams ended up scripting a dance-off for their sketch. It was like that episode of that drag show where everybody wore a kimono? But anyway, I was reminded of that during the festival weekend when I kept hearing the same songs played multiple times.
</p>
Some examples that I remember include:

- Heads Will Roll
- Show Me Love
- Pump Up The Jam
- Diet Coke
- Woops
- That Acid

<h2>The universe will bring us together again</h2>
<p>
On the way into the festival one day, I ran into this cool young girl named Skye. Her mom is a burner, this is her first festival, she had on a smile and a pair of boots haha bless. I gave her some Floptropican kandi and tried to give her beads but she didn't have any pockets. That's ok, I said, "the universe will bring us together again!" And I went on with my day. That night, as I was coming back to camp for the final time, who do I see but Skye? I call out her name, and it is her! I say, "The universe brought us together again!" and I gave her beads because she had a backpack with her this time. She may or may not have remembered me, but hopefully she'll make something with the beads that match her kandi bracelet.
</p>
<p>
I was called to give beads to another person who came in after me through security, this angelic tall figure with a cute sunglasses and pash combo. I PLUR'd her a bracelet and gave some beads as well. She was sweet, she kept saying "I don't have anything!" to give me, and I just replied, "That's ok, you have beads!" Her bf came up to us (I was literally wearing a speedo, bless their hearts) and I gave him some beads as well. After the festival, the algorithm algorithm'd and I found a tiktok video she made. I left a cheeky comment about the beads and dropped a follow, our digital world is so interesting. 
</p>
